The authentication flow, step by step:

Select your Identity Provider (IdP) Select an IdP that has your authentication data. SSOCircle is used as an public IdP for this sample project. We can either enable users to select an IdP (like what I did in this case) or we can configure and force an automatic means of IdP discovery.

Authenticate against the selected IdP This Authentication Service generates a SAML 2.0 authentication request, which is encoded and embedded into the URL for SSO service. After redirecting, you (user) must provide your credentials to authenticate against the selected IdP. So, please create an account in SSOCircle which is a very simple process or use my account (username: rezakhv - password: reza3004) to test the project.

Get back and see your login data The IdP returns the encoded SAML response to the browser. You will get back to the Authentication Service. If your identity is established by the IdP, you will be provided with app access and your profile data displayed in our Authentication Service.

Logout from your session You can now logout from the app. If enabled, you can also invoke the Single Logout (SLO) that invalidates client application sessions in addition to its own SSO session (IdP-side).
